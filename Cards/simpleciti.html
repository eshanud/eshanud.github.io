01 Problem

The right signal exists.
It just arrives after the damage is done.

12 active deals. The information exists — CRM, email, spreadsheet. The problem is the 20–30 minutes every morning spent reconstructing it to find the one deal on fire.

Critical 01

The Phase I ESA cascade

One missing environmental report blocks Credit Review, delays close, triggers rate lock renegotiation. Officer finds out when the lawyer emails — two days before closing.

Systemic 02

LTV buried in the deal

Every officer checks LTV first. Every platform requires clicking in to see it. 12 deals = 12 unnecessary clicks before triage even starts.

Systemic 03

Stage tells where, not whether

"Underwriting" on 8 deals = 8 different situations. Officers rebuild context every morning to separate urgent from idle.

04 Design System

Resolved before the first screen.

Token system locked first. Navy for authority, gold for financial signal moments, monospaced numerals throughout. JetBrains Mono for every number isn't aesthetic — it prevents misreads when scanning a column of dollar figures at speed. Monospaced numerals are how financial software earns trust.

04 Solution

Three zoom levels, one surface —
plus a risk signal nobody else has.

Level 01

Portfolio Health

4 KPIs. Total pipeline, active deals, avg LTV, closings this month. Macro read in 3 seconds.

Deal Triage

LTV color-coded. Stage pills. Close dates flagged amber at 7 days. Risk Score on every row.

Panel slides in on row click. Capital stack, doc checklist, timeline — pipeline stays visible.

06 What I'd Do Differently

Assumptions I got wrong — or diagnosed but didn't solve.

01 Critical gap

Multi-entity borrower structures

CRE deals above $5–7M involve layered ownership — LLC → Trust → individuals, sometimes foreign entities. My intake form assumed one borrower, one signature. That breaks on 40–60% of the target pipeline, with downstream pain in KYC/AML and document execution.

Fix needed Entity relationship mapping, multiple guarantors, dynamic signature workflows. Week 1 work.
02 Missed opportunity

Appraisal stress-testing

I named appraisal variance as a deal-killer, then designed nothing for it. Zero modeling capability for an 8–12% appraisal shortfall — the most common late-stage surprise in CRE lending.

Fix needed A non-destructive scenario panel recalculating LTV, DSCR, and capital stack impact.
03 Deferred problem

Credit committee / Submit for Approval workflow

The "Submit for Approval" button leads nowhere. Credit committee review is where approval latency actually lives — I stopped exactly at the handoff.

Fix needed Reviewer assignment, comment threads, version control, approval status visibility.
07 What I Learned

Domain knowledge isn't research overhead. It's the design work.

Consumer products optimize for delight. Enterprise tools must optimize for decision velocity.

  • Domain fluency turned a generic table into something credible. Column ordering is a domain problem, not an aesthetic one.
  • Cards looked right. They were structurally wrong for the workflow. Looking good isn't a validation signal.
  • Writing rationale for decisions revealed the holes. Articulating a decision is part of making a better one.

"The hardest part of design isn't craft. It's knowing which problem actually needs solving — and having the domain depth to see it first."

— The principle this whole project was built around
